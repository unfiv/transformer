### 2. What programming achievement are you most proud of?
I am particularly proud of a balancing system I developed for my own card game back in 2015. 

To solve the challenge of game balance, I "reinvented" integration testing by creating a **Monte Carlo-style simulation** where different AI agents played against each other thousands of times. I used median results and statistical analysis to fine-tune the mechanics. Additionally, I implemented a **deterministic pseudo-randomness system** to ensure reproducibility while maintaining unpredictable AI behavior. 

This data-driven approach significantly improved the game balance and player satisfaction, and it remains one of my most formative engineering experiences.

### 4. Handling Complex Systems & Sticking Points
When facing a complex, undocumented system (like a legacy A/B test logic), my approach is always methodical:

1.  **Impact Analysis:** My first step is a preliminary analysis to understand the scope.
2.  **Communication:** I immediately communicate with stakeholders to manage expectations regarding timelines and potential risks.
3.  **Collaboration:** I am not afraid to ask for help from more experienced colleagues. I believe that timely collaboration is far more efficient than "getting stuck" in isolation, and it often strengthens team bonds.
4.  **Estimation:** If no immediate guidance is available, I double my most conservative estimates to account for a deep dive into the codebase. 

My philosophy is that **every engineering problem is solvable** given enough time and a methodical approach, which has consistently led to successful outcomes in my past projects.
